R.A. 9344 - Juvenile Delinquency

The Republic Act 9344, also known as the "Juvenile Justice and Welfare Act of 2006" in the Philippines, is a law that focuses on the treatment and rehabilitation of children in conflict with the law. Here are key points about R.A. 9344:

1.Purpose: The law aims to promote a child-friendly justice system that ensures the well-being of children in conflict with the law. It recognizes the vulnerability of children and their potential for rehabilitation.

2.Age of Criminal Responsibility: R.A. 9344 raised the minimum age of criminal responsibility from 9 to 15 years old. Children below 15 years old are deemed as having discernment and are, therefore, exempted from criminal liability.

3.Diversion Programs: The law encourages the use of diversion programs rather than punitive measures. It emphasizes rehabilitation and reintegration into society for children in conflict with the law.

4Juvenile Justice and Welfare Council (JJWC): The JJWC is established to oversee the implementation of the law and ensure the protection of the rights of the child throughout the judicial process.

5.Child Detention Facilities: R.A. 9344 mandates the establishment of separate detention facilities for children in conflict with the law, ensuring that they are not detained with adult offenders.

As the implementation of the law evolves, the impact on the lives of juvenile offenders is likely to resonate far beyond the confines of legal proceedings, creating a ripple effect of positive change in the landscape of juvenile justice.
